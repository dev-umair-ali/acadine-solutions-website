Focused on fixing failed AI projects. We assess and fix AI implementations that are not delivering value — stabilize outcomes, rebuild on a foundation that works.

01Failure patterns

Why AI projects fail — and how we fix each one

Every rescue starts with pattern recognition. These are the failure modes we see most, paired with the corrective approach.

01

Business–Technology Misalignment

Initiatives launched without a crisp operational problem statement or measurable owner.

Corrective approach

Re-anchor scope to workflows and KPIs; retire features that do not map to outcomes.

02

Data Readiness Gaps

Fragmented sources, weak definitions, and unclear ownership undermine reliability.

Corrective approach

Establish definitions, access patterns, and quality checks before scaling automation.

03

Adoption and Change Risk

Teams bypass tools when workflows are harder than the old way.

Corrective approach

Redesign incentives, training, and support so new operating habits stick.

04

Operational Fragility

Pilot success that breaks under production volume, exceptions, or audits.

Corrective approach

Harden integrations, monitoring, and governance for steady-state operations.

02Assessment process

A systematic path from failure to recovery

Three phases, each with concrete deliverables — so you always know where things stand.

01
Phase 01

Diagnosis

Deep-dive into the failed implementation to isolate root causes — technical debt, data gaps, process mismatches, or adoption barriers.

Deliverables

  • Technical & data audit findings

02
Phase 02

Assessment

Evaluate what is salvageable. Score existing infrastructure, models, and integrations against the operational requirements that were missed.

Deliverables

  • Gap analysis vs. business requirements

03

Architect a recovery plan that addresses every failure mode — with near-term stabilization steps and a sustainable long-term path.

Deliverables
  • Recovery roadmap with milestones
  • Revised architecture & integration plan
  • Change management playbook
